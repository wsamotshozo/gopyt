  • DETERMINE YOUR TUITION GOAL

    • When setting a goal for your tuition payments, it is important to set a target that is realistic and that does not scare off your future supporters. It will be hard to determine all of your tuition needs at the time of signing up, but a good estimate can be found by looking at the average fees of the school you are looking to attend or the average current tuition costs (for those who aren’t yet sure of their choice of college). In your profile you can describe some of the needs and costs associated with your dream school and give any additional cost information that you have researched or have confirmed with your chosen school. If you don’t know yet exactly what the costs for your tuition will be then shoot for a smaller goal that is more attainable to help jumpstart your higher education finances.

      This goal can be used to finance any sort of education. The funds you raise could go towards paying for the fees of a higher education institution, a trade or technical school, any private or public school or towards paying for instructional classes. This site is for any educational costs. Here are some additional links on tuition costs and average school fees. Take a few minutes to confirm your tuition goals using the examples provided to help you:

      Example 1: It’s important to set your sights high when drawing up your tuition goals; don’t be afraid to push for the top. Set a big goal overall, but break it down into smaller, more manageable sums.

      Let’s say for example that your tuition goal is $30,000 that will go towards paying your college fees.

      $30,000 divided by 4 years of college = $7500 per year
      $7500 per year divided by 12 months = $625 per month

      $625 per month divided by 30 days = a daily goal of $21

      By creating smaller milestones like daily or monthly goals, you’ll make your target seem a lot more achievable, and you’ll be more able to canvas support from locals. If $21 a day is all you need, then that’s just $1 a day from 21 different sources!

      Example 2: Alternatively, you could break it down like this.

      $30,000 over 4 years broken down into 48 months = $625 a month
      If you can get 10 people to donate 62 dollars a month every month then you’ll already have hit your goal, not to mention the fact that the extra rewards offered by GOPYT will make it even easier for you to reach your monthly targets.

  • PROMOTING YOUR FUND

    • This is where the real work comes in. You will need to blast your friends and family with your profile page. Ask them to send your profile to anyone they know. You’ll have to reach out to your friends, family, church, local organizations, schools and any community outreach programs that can help spread the word. You should send out weekly reminders to keep people updated on your progress. Remember, people’s monthly donations are the key to helping you win additional rewards. You may also want to contact any charitable organizations in your area. In return for their pledge to support your cause, you might want to give up your time to help them too. You can visit local businesses and ask them to help promote your campaign. You could either ask them to sponsor you directly or to publicize your profile via their websites or social media. As another idea, you could ask local stores to put up donation jars for you. That way, you can collect the money raised each month and send it in to help you win the monthly GOPYT incentives. These are just some of the many ways you can back your campaign, why not try thinking of your own creative solutions to funding your future.
